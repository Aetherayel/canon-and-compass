# Clearing Patterns

Use this file when drafting or revising essays for `src/content/the-clearing`.

## Purpose

`The Clearing` is where the reader slows down enough to tell the truth again.

It is:

- pre-diagnostic
- witness-first
- anti-spectacle
- anti-panic
- Christ-centered

It is not:

- a worldview lecture
- a forest map
- a fruit-path treatment plan
- a foundations-style reinforcement layer

## Core Essay Types

Default essay types for `The Clearing`:

- threshold essays for readers who feel flooded, scattered, numb, or spiritually thin
- misnaming essays that expose false labels like discernment that is really fear
- false-urgency essays that break the spell of pressure and speed
- witness-training essays that teach the reader how to name reality truthfully
- Christ-attention essays that return the reader to Christ without spectacle
- quiet testimony essays that model honesty without drama
- bridge essays that prepare the reader to enter a forest or fruit path

## Drafting Template

This is a pattern, not a rigid outline:

1. Start with the felt condition.
2. Name what the reader is likely calling it.
3. Expose what may actually be happening underneath.
4. Show what the false pattern steals or distorts.
5. Reintroduce proportion, honesty, or scale.
6. Return attention to Christ as steady presence and true center.
7. End with a quieter next step, not a complete system.

## Voice Markers

Prefer:

- short opening paragraphs
- direct but unhurried prose
- gentle repetition
- sentences that clarify rather than impress
- language of attention, naming, witness, urgency, pressure, proportion, rest, honesty, presence

Calibrate toward:

- "Most people do not first need more explanation. They need quieter ground."
- "The pieces in The Clearing are not arguments. They are witness texts."
- "You don’t leave the Clearing with a plan. You leave with a steadier witness."

## Boundaries

Move the piece elsewhere if it becomes mainly:

- a defense of Christian metaphysics or epistemology
- a detailed analysis of a worldview climate
- a visible-fruit to hidden-root pathway
- a long doctrinal scaffold meant to hold under pressure

Those belong more naturally to `Pillars`, `The Forests`, or `Fruit Paths`.

## Title Patterns

Useful title shapes:

- `When ...`
- `Why ...`
- `You May Not Be ...`
- `The Difference Between ... and ...`
- `What If ...`
- `Christ Does Not ...`

These usually work best when they name a felt burden, false equation, or hidden pressure.

## Summary Patterns

Strong summaries often do one of these:

- name a condition and its hidden distortion
- contrast false urgency with truthful witness
- show what must break or soften before clarity returns

Examples:

- `When every burden feels urgent, the soul loses proportion.`
- `Comparison often assumes someone else's shape is the standard.`
- `Truthful witness begins when false urgency breaks and reality can be named again.`

## First Slate

Recommended initial clearing slate for the newer voice:

1. `When Everything Feels Spiritually Important at Once`
2. `When Discernment Becomes a Form of Control`
3. `How to Tell the Truth Before God Without Performing`
4. `Why Noise Can Feel Like Clarity`
5. `Christ Does Not Need Your Panic to Reach You`

Use these as anchors when expanding the section, even if the exact titles shift.
