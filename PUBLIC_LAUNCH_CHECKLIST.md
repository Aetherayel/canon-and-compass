# Public Launch Checklist

This checklist is for moving Canon & Compass from a personal or portfolio project into a public-facing discipleship site that strangers can browse with confidence.

## 1. Pastoral Boundaries

- Add visible care language on high-sensitivity pages about anxiety, panic, trauma, depression, addiction, abuse, grief, and suicidal thinking.
- Keep language clear: discipleship guidance is not diagnosis, psychotherapy, medical treatment, or crisis response.
- Point readers toward local church care, licensed counseling, and emergency support when appropriate.
- Review every pathway that names embodied symptoms and ask: does this sound like spiritual insight, or like a total explanation of someone’s suffering?

## 2. Scripture Policy

- Pick a translation policy and apply it consistently.
- Label quotations clearly in articles and callouts.
- Keep the required copyright/permission language visible for copyrighted translations.
- Standardize older content that still quotes Scripture without a translation marker.

## 3. Doctrinal Clarity

- Add a short statement of faith before broad public launch.
- State whether the project speaks from a specific church tradition, denomination, or broadly evangelical Protestant perspective.
- Clarify what authority the site claims and does not claim.
- Distinguish “this project argues” from “all faithful Christians must say.”

## 4. Author Trust Signals

- Add a concise author bio with name, role, and relevant formation.
- Explain whether Canon & Compass is a solo writing project, ministry initiative, or church-adjacent resource.
- Add a contact or feedback pathway that sets expectations for response.
- Decide how anonymous or personal testimony-heavy you want the site to be before it circulates publicly.

## 5. Content Integrity

- Do a line-by-line editorial pass for punctuation, spacing, encoding issues, and broken quotation marks.
- Fix visible corrupted characters such as mojibake before promotion.
- Review all slugs and filenames for public-facing polish.
- Check internal links, pathway cross-links, and citation references manually.

## 6. High-Claim Essays

- Review apologetics-heavy pieces for claims that need citations, sourcing, or softer framing.
- Flag phrases like “the only coherent framework,” “history proves,” or “science depends on” and decide whether you can defend them in public.
- Where you want stronger claims, add bibliography or notes.
- Where you do not want to maintain footnotes, reduce certainty and write with more explicit humility.

## 7. Sensitive Topic Review

- Review content touching mental health, abuse, trauma, sexuality, politics, and church hurt with extra care.
- Ask whether each piece leaves room for complexity, embodiment, and pastoral nuance.
- Remove any wording that could sound like blame toward sufferers.
- Ask whether a skeptical but fair reader would feel understood before being corrected.

## 8. Public Readiness QA

- Proofread homepage, About, The Clearing, two flagship Fruit Paths, and two flagship Foundations pieces first.
- Validate mobile readability and scanability on longer essays.
- Confirm metadata, titles, summaries, canonical URLs, sitemap, and RSS output.
- Check that contact email, copyright year, and domain references are current.

## 9. Recommended Immediate Next Steps

- Standardize Scripture labels across older MDX files.
- Add a statement of faith page or section.
- Continue softening over-totalized explanations in mental-health-adjacent content.
- Audit legacy filenames and decide whether to preserve them, rename them, or add redirects.
- Do one final human editorial pass on all launch-critical pages before promotion.
